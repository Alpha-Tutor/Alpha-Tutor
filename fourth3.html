"I Have a Dream"

On August 28, 1963, Martin Luther King Jr. stood in front of thousands of people at the Lincoln Memorial in Washington D.C. and gave his famous speech called "I Have a Dream." He talked about how he wanted everyone to be treated equally, no matter the color of their skin. Dr. King shared his vision of a future where people would not be judged by their appearance but by who they are inside. He dreamed of a world where children of all colors could play and live together peacefully.

Dr. King's speech touched the hearts of many Americans and people around the world. It became a powerful message of hope and a symbol of the civil rights movement. His words inspired people to stand up for justice and equality. Today, Martin Luther King Jr.'s "I Have a Dream" speech is still remembered as one of the most important speeches in American history, reminding us all of the dream of a better, fairer world for everyone.
